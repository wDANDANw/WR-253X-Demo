Background of the Manifesto

Published May 1, 2022

The central vision I want to convey in the manifesto is that "everyone should have equal rights and a chance to convert their original ideas and thoughts to technology-assisted designs" (techno-designs).

It is a part of a more excellent vision that "everyone's design should be valued and cherished in the information age." The information age is a historical period that started in the 1950s, and it is characterized by the rapid growth of the availability of information technologies. The information technologies, often represented by computers, enabled humans to convert their ideas into vivid, interactive artificial mediums, like games. Since then, the technology-enabled designers to create content with more sensical details (like visual details, sound details, or even haptic details) and possibilities of real-time responses. However, techno-designs discourage new designers due to the fantastic range of capabilities. It is becoming more complex and challenging to learn all the necessary skills to utilize the correct technologies. In this manifesto, I would argue my envision that we should make techno-designs more available to everyone by lowering the entrance level, and this would bring a new emergence of brilliant designs.
