Assumption 1: Efforts should guarantee returns

Published May 1, 2022

Designers should expect that they can get a techno-design after paying a certain quantity of effort, and they would be able to polish the design with more effort.

I will start with my first assumption: "everyone should have equal rights and chances to do something." The equality claims will implicitly imply that if one should spend a certain amount of effort, one should get at least a certain amount of return. This assumption concretes the obscure concept of "equal rights and chance" to a more measurable and controllable parameter: quantity of efforts. Then, I can make the first core vision of the manifesto that "designers should expect that they can get a techno-design after paying a certain quantity of effort, and they would be able to polish the design with more effort."
